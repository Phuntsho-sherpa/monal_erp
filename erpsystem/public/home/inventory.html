About

Inventory management refers to the process of tracking, controlling and optimizing a company's inventory of goods and materials. The main objective of inventory management is to ensure that a business has the right amount of inventory to meet customer demand while minimizing costs associated with storing and handling excess inventory.

Our Process

1.Monitoring inventory levels.
2.Forecasting demand.
3.Setting reorder points.
4.Managing the procurement process.
5.Implementing inventory control policies.
6.Using inventory management software to automate and streamline the process.
7.Conducting regular inventory audits.

Our Approach

1.ABC Analysis - Classifying inventory items based on their value and criticality.
2.Just-In-Time (JIT) - Minimizing inventory levels by ordering only what is needed.
3.Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) - Finding the optimal order quantity that minimizes total inventory costs.
4.First-In-First-Out (FIFO) - Managing inventory based on the order in which items are received.
5.Last-In-First-Out (LIFO) - Managing inventory based on the order in which items are sold.

Our Goal

1.To achieve a high inventory turnover rate.
2.To minimize stockouts and backorders.
3.To optimize inventory levels to reduce holding costs and increase cash flow.

Our Mission

1.To maintain adequate inventory levels to meet customer demand.
2.To minimize the costs associated with storing and handling excess inventory.
3.To improve the efficiency of the procurement and inventory management process.
